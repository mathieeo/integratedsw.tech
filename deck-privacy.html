Deck — Privacy Policy

Integrated Software Technologies Inc. · Last updated July 7, 2026

Short version: Deck collects nothing. It has no accounts, no analytics, and no third‑party trackers. Everything happens on your device.

What Deck stores

Deck is an SSH client. To connect to your servers, it saves the host details and credentials you enter (host address, port, username, and your password or private key). This information is stored only on your device, in the iOS Keychain, and is never transmitted to us or to anyone other than the servers you choose to connect to.

What Deck does not do

  • We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data.
  • We do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.
  • We do not include third‑party SDKs that collect data.
  • We have no servers that receive your data — your SSH sessions go directly from your device to the servers you configure.

Network connections

Deck connects only to the SSH servers you explicitly add. These connections are made directly from your device using standard, encrypted SSH. Deck may request permission to access devices on your local network, which is required to connect to servers on your Wi‑Fi (for example a home lab or office machine).

Face ID / Touch ID

If you enable the app lock, Deck uses Face ID or Touch ID solely to unlock the app on your device. This biometric check is handled entirely by iOS; Deck never sees or stores your biometric data.

Children

Deck is a developer tool and is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect any information from anyone.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date.
